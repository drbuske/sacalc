Item a.

Consider the series

\begin{equation*} \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{k-2}{k^4+1}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since the convergence or divergence of a series only depends on the behavior of the series for large values of \(k\text{,}\) we might examine the terms of this series more closely as \(k\) gets large. By computing the value of \(\frac{k-2}{k^4+1}\) for \(k=100\) and \(k=1000\text{,}\) explain why the terms \(\frac{k-2}{k^4+1}\) are essentially \(\frac{k}{k^4}\) when \(k\) is large.
