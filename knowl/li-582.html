Item 2.

  1. Let \(a_k = \frac{k^2-5}{k^3+k+2}\) and \(b_k = \frac{1}{k}\text{.}\) Then

    \begin{equation*} \lim_{k \rightarrow \infty} \frac{a_k}{b_k} = \lim_{k \rightarrow \infty} \frac{k(k^2-5)}{k^3+k+2} = 1 \end{equation*}

    so that the series diverges since \(\sum \frac{1}{k}\) diverges.

  2. Let \(a_k = \sin \left( \frac{1}{k}\right)\) and \(b_k = \frac{1}{k}\text{.}\) Then

    \begin{equation*} \lim_{k \rightarrow \infty} \frac{a_k}{b_k} = \lim_{k \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\sin \left( \frac{1}{k} \right)}{\frac{1}{k}} = 1 \end{equation*}

    since \(\frac{\sin x}{x} \rightarrow 1\) as \(x \rightarrow 0\text{.}\) Thus the series diverges since \(\sum \frac{1}{k}\) diverges.
