Solution 6.2.20.1.

We start by determining where \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) intersect. But \(x^2+6=4x+6\) has solutions \(x=0,4\text{.}\) So the curves intersect at \((0,6)\) and \((4,22)\text{.}\) Note also that \(g(x)\) is above \(f(x)\text{.}\) So the side length of each square cross-section of \(S\) at \(x\) is \(g(x)-f(x) = 4x-x^2\text{.}\) This gives the area of a cross-section at \(x\) to be \(A(x) = (4x-x^2)^2\text{.}\)

We can then integrate to determine the volume of \(S\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} {\text{Volume}} = \mathstrut \amp \int_a^b A(x) \ dx \\ = \mathstrut \amp \int_0^4 (4x-x^2)^2 \ dx\\ = \mathstrut \amp \frac{103}{3}. \end{align*}
