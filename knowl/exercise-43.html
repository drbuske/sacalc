Exercise 7.

A depleted harmonic series is a series obtained from \(\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{k}\) by removing any term \(1/k\) if a given digit, say \(9\text{,}\) appears in the decimal expansion of \(k\text{.}\) Argue that this depleted harmonic series converges by answering the following.

  1. How many whole numbers \(k\) have \(d\) digits?

  2. How many \(d\)-digits whole numbers \(h(d)\) do not contain \(9\) as one or more of their digits?

  3. What is the smallest \(d\)-digit number \(m(d)\text{?}\)

  4. Explain why the deleted harmonic series is bounded by \(\sum_{d=1}^{\infty} \frac{h(d)}{m(d)}\text{.}\)

  5. Show that \(\sum_{d=1}^{\infty} \frac{h(d)}{m(d)}\) converges.
