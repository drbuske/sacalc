Exercise 6.

In this exercise, our goal is to estimate \(\sqrt{2}\) by finding a polynomial that approximates the function \(f(x) = \sqrt{1+x}\text{,}\) and then evaluating that polynomial at \(x=1\text{.}\)

  1. Find the linearization, \(L(x)\text{,}\) to \(f(x)\) at \(x=0\text{.}\) Then, evaluate \(L(1)\text{.}\) Recall: The graph of the local linearization is the tangent line at \(x=0\text{.}\)

  2. Find \(P_1(x)\text{,}\) the first degree Taylor polynomial at \(x=0\) to \(f(x)\text{.}\) Then, evaluate \(P_1(1)\text{.}\) What do you notice?

  3. Find \(P_2(x)\) and \(P_3(x)\text{,}\) the second and third degree Taylor polynomials at \(x=0\) to \(f(x)\text{.}\) Then, evaluate both \(P_2(1)\) and \(P_3(1)\text{.}\) Which one better estimates \(\sqrt{2}\text{?}\)
