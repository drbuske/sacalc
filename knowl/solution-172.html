Solution 2.7.3.1.1.

We differentiate implicitly with respect to \(x\text{.}\) This gives

\begin{equation*} 3 x^{2} y - x^{3} \, \frac{dy}{dx} - 1 - 2 \, \frac{dy}{dx} = 0, \end{equation*}

or

\begin{equation*} (x^{3}-2) \, \frac{dy}{dx} = 1-3x^{2}y, \end{equation*}

and so

\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1 - 3 x^{2} y}{x^{3} - 2}. \end{equation*}
