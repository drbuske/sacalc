Consider the integral \(\displaystyle \int \frac{2}{x^2\sqrt{x^2-9}}\) for \(x > 3\text{.}\)

  1. If \(x=3\sec\theta\) for \(0 < \theta < \frac{\pi}{2}\text{,}\) what are the possible values of \(x\text{?}\) Is \(x\) a one-to-one function of \(\theta\text{?}\)

  2. For \(0 < \theta < \frac{\pi}{2}\text{,}\) is \(\tan\theta\) positive or negative?

  3. For \(x > 3\text{,}\) transform the integral \(\displaystyle \int \frac{2}{x^2\sqrt{x^2-9}}\) to one in the variable \(\theta\text{.}\)

  4. Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int \frac{2}{x^2\sqrt{x^2-9}}\) for \(x > 3\text{.}\)
