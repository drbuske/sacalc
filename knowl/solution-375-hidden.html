Over the interval \([0,\pi]\text{,}\) the graphs \(y = 9\sin x\) and \(y = 10 \cos x\) intersect when \(9\sin x = 10 \cos x\text{,}\) i.e., \(\tan x = \frac{10}{9}\text{,}\) i.e., \(x=\tan^{-1}(\frac{10}{9})=0.83798122500839\text{.}\) Let \(t = \tan^{-1}(\frac{10}{9})\text{.}\) Over the interval \(\left[0, t\right]\text{,}\) the graph of \(y = 9\sin x\) lies below that of \(y = 10 \cos x\text{.}\) Hence, the area between the two curves is \(\int_0^{ t}(10 \cos x-9 \sin x)dx\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} \amp =\left.(10\sin x+9\cos x)\right|_0^{ t} \cr \amp =\left(10\sin t+9\cos t\right)-(10\cdot 0+9\cdot 1)\cr \amp =\left(10\sin t+9\cos t\right)-9 \end{array} \end{equation*}
Since \(t = \tan^{-1}(\frac{10}{9})\text{,}\) we have \(\tan t = \frac{10}{9}\text{.}\) Considering a rightangle triangle with an angle which opposite side has length 10 and adjacent side has length 9, we see that the hypotenues is \(\sqrt{181}\text{.}\) Thus \(\sin t = \frac{10}{\sqrt{181}}\) and \(\cos t = \frac{9}{\sqrt{181}}\text{.}\) \(10\sin t+9\cos t = \frac{10^2}{\sqrt{181}} + \frac{9^2}{\sqrt{181}} = \frac{10^2+9^2}{\sqrt{181}} = \frac{181}{\sqrt{181}}=\sqrt{181}\text{.}\) Thus, \(\int_0^{ t}(10 \cos x-9 \sin x)dx= \sqrt{181}-9.\) Over the interval \(\left[ t,\pi\right]\text{,}\) the graph of \(y = 9\sin x\) lies above that of \(y = 10 \cos x\text{.}\) Hence, the area between the two curves is \(\int^\pi_{ t}(9\sin x - 10 \cos x)dx\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} \amp =-\left.(10\sin x+9\cos x)\right|^\pi_{ t} \cr \amp =9 + \sqrt{181} \end{array} \end{equation*}
Thus the total area is the sum of the above two integrals, which is \(2\sqrt{181}\text{.}\)
