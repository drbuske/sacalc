Exercise 6.

A polar curve \(r=f(\theta)\) can be written in parametric form

\begin{align*} x= \mathstrut \amp r\cos\theta = f(\theta)\cos\theta\\ y = \mathstrut \amp r\sin\theta = f(\theta)\sin\theta \end{align*}

where we treat \(\theta\) as the parameter. The derivative is given by

\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy/d\theta}{dx/d\theta}\text{.} \end{equation*}

  1. Find the slope of the tangent line to \(r=\cos\theta\) at \(\left( 2, \frac{\pi}{3} \right)\text{.}\)

  2. Find the slope of the tangent line to the polar curve \(r=\theta\) at the point given by \(\theta = \frac{\pi}{2}\text{.}\)

  3. Find the points on the interval \(-\pi \leq \theta \leq \pi\) at which the cardioid \(r=1-\cos\theta\) has a vertical or horizontal tangent line.
