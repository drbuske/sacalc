\(\sin(x) = x - \frac1{3!} x^3 + \frac1{5!} x^5 - \frac1{7!} x^7 + \cdots\) Thus, substituting \(7 x\) into this series, we get
\begin{equation*} \sin(7 x) = 7 x - \frac{7^3}{3!} x^3 + \frac{7^5}{5!} x^5 - \frac{7^7}{7!} x^7 + \cdots. \end{equation*}
Finally, multiplying by \(x^{5}\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} x^{5} \sin(7 x) = 7 x^{6} - \frac{7^3}{3!} x^{8} + \frac{7^5}{5!} x^{10} - \frac{7^7}{7!} x^{12} + \cdots. \end{equation*}
