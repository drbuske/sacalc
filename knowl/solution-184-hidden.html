\(f(x)=\ln(x/5)\) and \(g(x)=x^2-25\text{,}\) so \(f(5)=0\) and \(g(5)=0\) and l'Hopital's rule can be used. To apply l'Hopital's rule, we first find \(f'(x)=1/x\) and \(g'(x)=2x\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \rightarrow 5} \frac{\ln(x/5)}{x^2-25} = \lim_{x \rightarrow 5} \frac{1/x}{2x}=\lim_{x \rightarrow 5} \frac{1}{2x^2}=\frac{1}{50}. \end{equation*}
