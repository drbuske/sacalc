Preview Activity 7.4.1.

  1. Compute the value of the improper integral \(\int_1^{\infty} \frac{1}{x^2} \ dx\text{.}\)

  2. Using \(n=5\text{,}\) sketch the right Riemann sum \(R_5\) for \(\int_1^6 \frac{1}{x^2} \ dx\text{.}\) Is \(R_5\) an underestimate or an overestimate or neither?

  3. Compare \(1+R_5\) (as found above) with \(\sum_{k=1}^6 \frac{1}{k^2}\text{.}\)

  4. For any \(n\text{,}\) how do \(1+R_n\) and \(\int_1^{n+1} \frac{1}{x^2} \ dx\) compare? By extension, how does \(\sum_{k=1}^{n+1} \frac{1}{k^2}\) compare to \(\int_1^{\infty} \frac{1}{x^2} \ dx\) for any \(n\text{?}\)

  5. Discuss the convergence or divergence of \(\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{k^2}\text{.}\)
