Start with the Maclaurin series for \(e^x\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} e^x = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{1}{k!} x^k\text{.} \end{equation*}

Then substitute \(x=-t^2\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} e^{-t^2} = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^k}{k!} t^{2k}\text{.} \end{equation*}

We can then integrate term by term to find

\begin{align*} \erf (x) \amp = \frac{2}{\sqrt{\pi}} \int_0^x \left[ \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-t^2)^k}{k!} \right] \ dt\\ \amp = \frac{2}{\sqrt{\pi}} \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (-1)^k \frac{x^{2k+1}}{(2k+1)k!} \end{align*}

So

\begin{align*} \erf \left( \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\right) \amp = \frac{2}{\sqrt{\pi}} \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (-1)^k \frac{ \left( \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \right)^{2k+1}}{(2k+1)k!}\\ \amp = \frac{2}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (-1)^k \frac{1}{(2k+1)2^k k!}\\ \amp = \sqrt{ \frac{2}{\pi}} \left( 1 - \frac{1}{3 \cdot 2} + \frac{1}{5 \cdot 2^2 \cdot 2} - \frac{1}{7 \cdot 2^3 \cdot 3!} + \frac{1}{9 \cdot 2^4 \cdot 4!} - \cdots \right) \end{align*}

This is an alternating series. If we keep 5 terms, we get \(0.68271\) with an error between \(0\) and the first dropped term, which is minus

\begin{equation*} \sqrt{ \frac{2}{\pi}} \frac{ 1}{11 \cdot 2^5 \cdot 5!} < 2 \times 10^{-5}\text{.} \end{equation*}
