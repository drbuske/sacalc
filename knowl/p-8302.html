  1. Consider the series

    \begin{equation*} \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{k-2}{k^4+1}\text{.} \end{equation*}

    Since the convergence or divergence of a series only depends on the behavior of the series for large values of \(k\text{,}\) we might examine the terms of this series more closely as \(k\) gets large. By computing the value of \(\frac{k-2}{k^4+1}\) for \(k=100\) and \(k=1000\text{,}\) explain why the terms \(\frac{k-2}{k^4+1}\) are essentially \(\frac{k}{k^4}\) when \(k\) is large.

  2. This gives us the sense that the series \(\sum \frac{k-2}{k^4+1}\) should behave the same way that \(\sum \frac{k}{k^4} = \sum \frac{1}{k^3}\) behaves. Does \(\sum \frac{1}{k^3}\) converge or diverge? Why?

  3. Explain why for all \(k \geq 2\text{,}\) \(0 \leq \frac{k-2}{k^4+1} \leq \frac{k}{k^4} = \frac{1}{k^3}\text{.}\)

  4. Suppose that \(S_n = \sum_{k=1}^n \frac{k-2}{k^4+1}\) and \(T_n = \sum_{k=1}^n \frac{1}{k^3}\) are partial sums for the series \(\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{k-2}{k^2+1}\) and \(\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{k^3}\) respectively. Explain why we have \(S_n \leq T_n\) for all values of \(n \geq 1\text{.}\)

  5. Does the sequence \(\{ T_n \}\) converge or diverge? Why?

  6. We now so that the series \(\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{k-2}{k^4+1}\) is convergent by showing the sequence of partial sums \(\{S_n\}\) converge. Suppose that \(T = \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} T_n\text{.}\) Then, since each term in the partial sum \(T_n\) is positive, we have that \(\{ T_n \}\) is an increasing sequence and \(T_n \leq T\) for all \(n\text{.}\) Show that the sequence \(\{S_n\}\) is monotone and bounded and hence also converges.
