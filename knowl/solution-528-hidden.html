\begin{equation*} \cos(x) = 1 - {x^2\over2!} + {x^4\over4!} - \cdots, \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} e^{-x^2} = 1 - x^2 + {x^4\over2!} - {x^6\over3!} +\cdots. \end{equation*}
We note that the leading term in both is 1, and that subsequent terms for both are even powers of \(x\) (so that the functions are both even functions!). By comparing the sizes of the terms after the 1 (and remembering that for small \(x\text{,}\) \(x^6 \lt x^4 \lt x^2\text{,}\) we can see that \(\cos(x)\) is the larger function.
