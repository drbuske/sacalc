When \(k\) is very large, the constant 2 in the numerator is negligible when compared to \(k\text{,}\) and the constant 1 in the denominator is negligible when compared to \(k^4\text{.}\) So for large \(k\text{,}\) the numerator looks like \(k\) and the denominator looks like \(k^4\text{.}\) Thus, the fraction \(\frac{k-2}{k^4+1}\) looks like \(\frac{k}{k^4}\) for large \(k\text{.}\)
