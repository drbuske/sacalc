The idea behind the direct comparison test is very intuitive. If a series \(\sum a_k\) has terms that are all (beyond a certain \(k\)) smaller than the corresponding terms in a series \(\sum b_k\) that we know converges, then the unknown series \(\sum a_k\) must also converge, because it is "smaller" than something finite. Thus, we can use the first bullet point above to prove that a series (\(\sum a_k\)) converges.
