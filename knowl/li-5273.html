Item a.

To check for convergence, we apply the ratio test. Here \(a_k = \frac{x^k}{k!}\) so

\begin{align*} \lim_{k \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\left| a_{k+1}\right|}{\left| a_k \right|} \amp = \lim_{k \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\left| \frac{x^{k+1}}{(k+1)!} \right|}{\left| \frac{x^k}{k!} \right|}\\ \amp = \lim_{k \rightarrow \infty} \left| \frac{x^{k+1}}{(k+1)!} \cdot \frac{k!}{x^k} \right|\\ \amp = \lim_{k \rightarrow \infty} \left| \frac{x^k\cdot x}{(k+1)\cdot k!} \cdot \frac{k!}{x^k} \right|\\ \amp = \lim_{k \rightarrow \infty} \left| \frac{x}{k+1} \right|\\ \amp = \left| x \right| \lim_{k \rightarrow \infty} \frac{1}{k+1}\\ \amp = 0 < 1 \end{align*}

for all values of \(x\text{.}\) So the series converges for all real numbers \(x\) and the interval of convergence is \((-\infty, \infty)\text{.}\)
