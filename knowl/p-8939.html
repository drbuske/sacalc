To find the interval of convergence of the power series \(\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} c_k (x-a)^k\text{,}\) calculate

\begin{equation*} L = \lim_{k \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\left| c_{k+1} \right|}{\left| c_k \right|} \text{.} \end{equation*}

  • If \(L=0\text{,}\) then the power series converges on \((-\infty,\infty)\text{.}\)

  • If \(L=\infty\text{,}\) then the power series converges only at \(x=a\text{.}\)

  • If \(L\) is finite and nonzero, then the power series converges on the interval \(\left( a-\frac{1}{L}, a+\frac{1}{L}\right)\text{.}\) The series may or may not converge at each endpoint of this interval. Convergence at these endpoints must be checked separately.
