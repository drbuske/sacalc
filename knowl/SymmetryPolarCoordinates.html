Symmetry tests in polar coordinates.

  • A graph has symmetry with respect to the polar axis if, whenever \((r,\theta)\) is on the graph, so is the point \((r,-\theta)\text{.}\)

  • A graph has symmetry with respect to the vertical axis if, whenever \((r,\theta)\) is on the graph, so is the point \((-r,-\theta)\text{.}\)

  • A graph has symmetry with respect to the pole if, whenever \((r,\theta)\) is on the graph, so is the point \((r,\pi+\theta)\text{.}\)
