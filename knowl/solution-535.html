Solution 9.1.5.6.1.

(a) If we have \(t\ge 0\text{,}\) then \(x\ge 6\) and \(y\ge 3\text{,}\) so we're looking at the part of the line to the right of and above \((6,3)\text{.}\) Thus \(\left(-2,-5\right)\) is not on this section of the line, \(\left(22,19\right)\) is, and \(\left(34,31\right)\) is. Any additional points must have \(x\ge6\) and \(y\ge3\text{,}\) and must lie on the line. (b) We find the endpoints of the line segment obtained by restricting \(t\) to \(-4\le t\le -3\) we can plug \(t=-4\) and \(t=-3\) into the equations of the line, getting \(\left(-10,-13\right)\) and \(\left(-6,-9\right)\text{.}\) (c) To get the part of the line above the \(x\)-axis, we need \(3+4t > 0\text{.}\) This gives the interval \(\left(-0.75,\infty \right)\text{.}\)
