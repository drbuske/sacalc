Answer 8.2.3.6.1.

  1. \(L(x) = \frac{1}{2}x+1\text{;}\) \(L(1) = 1.5\)

  2. \(P_1(x) = 1+\frac{1}{2}x\text{;}\) \(P_1(1) = 1.5\text{;}\) this is the same as \(L(1)\) since \(L(x)\) and \(P_1(x)\) are identical.

  3. \(P_2(x) = 1+\frac{1}{2}x-\frac{1}{8}x^2\text{;}\) \(P_2(1) = 1.375\text{;}\) \(P_3(x) = 1+\frac{1}{2}x-\frac{1}{8}x^2+\frac{1}{16}x^3\text{;}\) \(P_3(1) = 1.4375\text{;}\) \(P_3(1)\) is the better estimate.
