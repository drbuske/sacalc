With \(r=4\) and \(\theta=3\pi/2\text{,}\) we find \(x=r\cos \theta = 4\cdot \cos(3\pi/2) = 0\) and \(y=r\sin \theta = 4\cdot \sin(3\pi/2) = -4\text{.}\) Thus, for \((r,\theta) = (4, 3\pi/2)\text{,}\) the rectangular coordinates are \((0, -4)\text{.}\) B. With \(r=4\) and \(\theta=\pi/2\text{,}\) we find \(x=r\cos \theta = 4\cdot \cos(\pi/2) = 0\) and \(y=r\sin \theta = 4\cdot \sin(\pi/2) = 4\text{.}\) Thus, for \((r,\theta) = (4, \pi/2)\text{,}\) the rectangular coordinates are \((0, 4)\text{.}\)
