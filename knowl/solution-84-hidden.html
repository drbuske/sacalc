\(f(150) = 82\) and \(f'(150) = 4\) tell us that at \(x = 150\text{,}\) the value of the function is 82 and the function is increasing at a rate of 4 units for a unit increase in \(x\text{.}\) Since we increase \(x\) by -0.5 units in going from 150 to 149.5, the value of the function goes up by approximately \(-0.5 \cdot 4 = -2\) units, so
\begin{equation*} f(149.5) \approx 82 - 0.5 \cdot 4 = 82 - 2 = 80. \end{equation*}
