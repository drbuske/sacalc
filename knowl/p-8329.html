Our goal is to build the inequality

\begin{equation*} \frac{3k+2}{4k^2-3} \geq \cdots \geq 0 \end{equation*}

where the middle is filled in with a series that we know diverges. To make a fraction smaller, we should decrease the numerator and increase the denominator. So we can build the inequality

\begin{equation*} \frac{3k+2}{4k^2-3} \geq \frac{3k}{4k^2-3} \geq \frac{3k}{4k^2} \geq \frac{3}{4k} \geq 0\text{.} \end{equation*}

Now since \(\sum \frac{3}{4n}\) diverges, then \(\sum \frac{3k+2}{4k^2-3}\) also diverges by the direct comparison test.
