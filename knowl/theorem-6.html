Theorem 1.4.18. Arc Length of a Parametric Curve.

Consider the plane curve defined by the parametric equations

\begin{equation*} x=x(t), \ y=y(t), \ a \leq t \leq b \end{equation*}

and assume that \(x(t)\) and \(y(t)\) are differentiable functions of \(t\text{.}\) Then the arc length of this curve is given by

\begin{equation} s=\int_a^b \sqrt{\left( \frac{dx}{dt}\right)^2 + \left( \frac{dy}{dt} \right)^2} \ dt\text{.}\tag{1.11} \end{equation}
