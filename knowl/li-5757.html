Item b.

First, square both sides of the equation. This gives \(r^2=9\text{.}\) Next replace \(r^2\) with \(x^2+y^2\text{.}\) This gives the equation \(x^2+y^2=9\text{,}\) which is the equation of a circle centered at the origin with radius 3. (Note: when squaring both sides of an equation it is possible to introduce new points unintentionally. This should always be taken into consideration. However, in this case we do not introduce new points. For example, \(\left(-3,\frac{\pi}{3}\right)\) is the same point as \(\left( 3,\frac{4\pi}{3}\right)\text{.}\))
