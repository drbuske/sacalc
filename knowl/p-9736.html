Imagine going on a bicycle ride through the country. The tires stay in contact with the road and rotate in a predictable pattern. Now suppose a very determined ant is tired after a long day and wants to get home. So he hangs onto the side of the tire and gets a free ride. The path that this ant travels down a straight road is called a cycloid (FigureÂ 9.1.17). A cycloid generated by a circle (or bicycle wheel) of radius \(a\) is given by the parametric equations

\begin{equation} x(t) = a(t-\sin t), \ y(t) = a(1-\cos t)\text{.}\tag{9.1} \end{equation}
