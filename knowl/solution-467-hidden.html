1. Let \(b_k = \frac{k^2+1}{k^4+2k+2}\) and \(a_k = \frac{1}{k^2}\text{.}\) Then

    \begin{align*} \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{b_k}{a_k} \amp = \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{\frac{k^2+1}{k^4+2k+2}}{\frac{1}{k^2}}\\ \amp = \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{k^4+k^2}{k^4+2k+2}\\ \amp = \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{1+\frac{1}{k^2}}{1+\frac{2}{k^3}+\frac{2}{k^4}}\\ \amp = 1\text{.} \end{align*}

    Since \(\lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{b_k}{a_k}\) is a finite positive constant, the Limit Comparison Test shows that \(\sum \frac{1}{k^2}\) and \(\sum \frac{k^2+1}{k^4+2k+2}\) either both converge or both diverge. We know that \(\sum \frac{1}{k^2}\) is a \(p\)-series with \(p > 1\) and so \(\sum \frac{1}{k^2}\) converges. Therefore, the series \(\sum \frac{k^2+1}{k^4+2k+2}\) also converges.

    1. Let \(a_k = \frac{k^2-5}{k^3+k+2}\) and \(b_k = \frac{1}{k}\text{.}\) Then

      \begin{equation*} \lim_{k \rightarrow \infty} \frac{a_k}{b_k} = \lim_{k \rightarrow \infty} \frac{k(k^2-5)}{k^3+k+2} = 1 \end{equation*}

      so that the series diverges since \(\sum \frac{1}{k}\) diverges.

    2. Let \(a_k = \sin \left( \frac{1}{k}\right)\) and \(b_k = \frac{1}{k}\text{.}\) Then

      \begin{equation*} \lim_{k \rightarrow \infty} \frac{a_k}{b_k} = \lim_{k \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\sin \left( \frac{1}{k} \right)}{\frac{1}{k}} = 1 \end{equation*}

      since \(\frac{\sin x}{x} \rightarrow 1\) as \(x \rightarrow 0\text{.}\) Thus the series diverges since \(\sum \frac{1}{k}\) diverges.
