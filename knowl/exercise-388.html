Exercise 5.

Consider the function \(f(x) = e^x\text{.}\)

  1. Find the \(6\)th degree Taylor polynomial \(P_6(x)\) centered at \(x=0\) for \(f(x)\text{.}\)

  2. Plot \(f(x)\) and \(P_2(x)\text{,}\) the second degree Taylor polynomial, centered at \(x=0\) for \(f(x)\) on the interval \([-1.5,1.5]\text{.}\)

  3. Determine the coefficient of \(x^k\) in the \(k\)th degree Taylor polynomial \(P_k(x)\) centered at \(x=0\) for \(f(x)\text{.}\)

  4. How well does \(P_2(1)\) estimate the value of \(e\text{?}\) \(P_4(1)\text{?}\) \(P_6(1)\text{?}\) Suggest a means to determine even better estimates for the value of \(e\text{.}\)
