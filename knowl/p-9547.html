The Taylor series for \(\cos(x)\) about \(x=0\) is

\begin{equation*} \cos(x) = 1 - \frac{x^2}{2!} + \frac{x^4}{4!} - \cdots\text{.} \end{equation*}

By composing with \(x^2\) and subtracting the resulting series from \(1\text{,}\) we obtain

\begin{equation*} 1-\cos(x^2) = 1- \left( 1 - \frac{x^4}{2!} + \frac{x^8}{4!} - \cdots \right) = \frac{x^4}{2!} - \frac{x^8}{4!} + \cdots\text{.} \end{equation*}

So,

\begin{align*} \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{1-\cos(x^2)}{x^4} \amp = \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{ \frac{x^4}{2!} - \frac{x^8}{4!} + \cdots}{x^4}\\ \amp = \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{2} - \frac{x^4}{4!} + \cdots\\ \amp = \frac{1}{2} \end{align*}
