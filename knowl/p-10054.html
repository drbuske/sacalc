Multiply both sides of the equation by \(r\text{.}\) This leads to \(r^2=6r\cos\theta - 8r\sin\theta\text{.}\) Next use the formulas

\begin{equation*} r^2=x^2+y^2, \ x=r\cos\theta, \ y=r\sin\theta\text{.} \end{equation*}

This gives

\begin{align*} r^2 = \mathstrut \amp 6(r\cos\theta) - 8(r\sin\theta)\\ x^2+y^2 = \mathstrut \amp 6x-8y. \end{align*}

To put this equation into standard form, first move the variables from the right-hand side of the equation to the left-hand side, then complete the square.

\begin{align*} x^2+y^2 = \mathstrut \amp 6x-8y\\ x^2-6x+y^2+8y = \mathstrut \amp 0\\ (x^2-6x)+(y^2+8y) = \mathstrut \amp 0\\ (x^2-6x+9)+(y^2+8y+16) = \mathstrut \amp 9+16\\ (x-3)^2+(y+4)^2 = \mathstrut \amp 25. \end{align*}

This is the equation of a circle with center at \((3,-4)\) and radius \(5\text{.}\) Notice that the circle passes through the origin since the center is \(5\) units away.
