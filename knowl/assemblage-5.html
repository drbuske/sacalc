Basic Convergence Properties of Series.

  • Changing a finite number of terms in a series does not change whether or not the series converges. However, it can change the number to which the series converges.

  • If \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n\) and \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n\) converge and \(k\) is constant, then:

    • \begin{equation*} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (a_n+b_n) = \left( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n \right) + \left ( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n \right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
    • \begin{equation*} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (ka_n) = k\left( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n \right)\text{.} \end{equation*}

  • If \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n\) does not converge, and \(k \neq 0\) is a constant, then \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (ka_n)\) also diverges.
