Solution 8.2.3.3.1.

\begin{equation*} g(x) = g(6) + g'(6)(x-6) + \frac{g''(6)}{2!}(x-6)^2 +\frac{g'''(6)}{3!}(x-6)^3+\cdots \end{equation*}
Substituting gives
\begin{equation*} g(x) = 2 + 5(x-6) + \frac{4}{2!}(x-6)^2 +\frac{-4}{3!}(x-6)^3+\cdots \end{equation*}
(a) The degree 2 Taylor polynomial, \(P_2 (x)\text{,}\) is obtained by truncating after the \((x-6)^2\) term:
\begin{equation*} P_2(x) = 2+5\!\left(x-6\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 4\!\left(x-6\right)^{2}. \end{equation*}
(b) The degree 3 Taylor polynomial, \(P_3 (x)\text{,}\) is obtained by truncating after the \((x-6)^3\) term:
\begin{equation*} P_3 (x) = 2+5\!\left(x-6\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 4\!\left(x-6\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{3!}\!\left(-4\right)\!\left(x-6\right)^{3} \end{equation*}
(c) Substitute \(x=5.9\) into the Taylor polynomial of degree 2:
\begin{equation*} P_2(4.9) = 2+5\!\left(5.9-6\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 4\!\left(5.9-6\right)^{2} = 1.52. \end{equation*}
From the Taylor polynomial of degree 3, we obtain
\begin{equation*} P_3(4.9) = 2+5\!\left(5.9-6\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 4\!\left(5.9-6\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{3!}\!\left(-4\right)\!\left(5.9-6\right)^{3} = 1.52067. \end{equation*}
