The cycloid in Figure (9.4) is defined by the equations

\begin{equation*} x(t) = t-\sin(t) \text{ and } y(t) = 1-\cos(t)\text{.} \end{equation*}

We aim to determine the length of this curve for \(0 \leq t \leq 2\pi\) (one period). We begin by partitioning the interval \([0,2\pi]\) into \(n\) subintervals of equal length and let \(0 = t_0 < t_1 < t_2 < \cdots < t_n = 2\pi\) be the endpoints of the subintervals. Shown in Figure 9.2.17 is the case of \(n=4\text{.}\) We approximate the length of the curve on each subinterval with the length of the segment connecting the endpoints.

  1. Write a formula for the length of the line segment that connects the endpoints \((x(t_{i-1}), y(t_{i-1}))\) and \((x(t_i), y(t_i))\) of the \(i\)th subinterval \([t_{i-1},t_i]\text{.}\) (This length is our approximation of the length of the curve on this interval.)

  2. Use your formula in (a) to write a sum that adds all \(n\) of the approximations to the lengths on each subinterval.

  3. True or False? For \(\Delta t = t_i - t_{i-1}\text{,}\)

    \begin{gather*} \sum_{i=1}^n \sqrt{[(x(t_{i})-x(t_{i-1})]^2 + [y(t_{i})-y(t_{i-1})]^2} \\ = \sum_{i=1}^n \sqrt{\left(\frac{x(t_{i})-x(t_{i-1})}{\Delta t}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{y(t_{i})-y(t_{i-1})}{\Delta t}\right)^2} \cdot \Delta t \end{gather*}

  4. What do we need to do with the sum in part (c) in order to obtain the exact value of the length of the graph of the curve on \([0,2\pi]\text{?}\) What integral does this produce? Evaluate, using WolframAlpha, this definite integral to find the length of the cycloid on \([0,2\pi]\text{.}\)
