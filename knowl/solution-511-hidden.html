\(n^{\rm th}\) degree Taylor polynomial about \(x=0\) approximating \(\cos\!\left(x\right)\) is \(|E_n|\leq \frac{M\cdot|x-0|^{n+1}}{(n+1)!}\text{,}\) where the \(n^{\rm th}\) derivative of \(\cos\!\left(x\right)\) is in magnitude less than or equal to \(M\) on \(0 \leq x \leq 1\text{.}\) Now the derivatives of \(\cos\!\left(x\right)\) are simply \(\cos\!\left(x\right)\text{,}\) \(\sin\!\left(x\right)\text{,}\) \(-\cos\!\left(x\right)\text{,}\) and \(-\sin\!\left(x\right)\text{.}\) The largest magnitude these ever take is 1, so \(M = 1\text{,}\) and thus \(|E_n| \leq \frac{|x|^{n+1}}{(n+1)!}\leq \frac{1.4^{n+1}}{(n+1)!}\text{.}\)
