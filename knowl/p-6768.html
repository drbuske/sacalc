To calculate the volume of the entire solid, we then add the volumes of all the shells and obtain

\begin{equation*} V \approx \sum_{i=1}^n 2\pi x_i f(x_i) \Delta x\text{.} \end{equation*}

Here we have a Riemann sum for the function \(2\pi x f(x)\text{.}\) Letting the number of shells increase without bound gives us

\begin{equation*} V = \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} 2\pi x_i f(x_i) \Delta x = \int_a^b 2\pi x f(x) \ dx\text{.} \end{equation*}
