Differentiation and Integration of Power Series.

Suppose \(f(x)\) has a power series expansion

\begin{equation} f(x) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} c_kx^k\tag{3.9} \end{equation}

and that the series converges absolutely to \(f(x)\) on the interval \((-R,R)\text{.}\) Then,

  • the power series obtained by differentiating Equation (3.9) term by term converges absolutely to \(f'(x)\) on \((-R,R)\text{.}\) That is,

    \begin{equation} f'(x) = \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} kc_kx^{k-1}\tag{3.10} \end{equation}

    for \(|x| < R\text{.}\)

  • the power series obtained by integrating Equation (3.9) term by term converges absolutely on the interval \((-R,R)\) and

    \begin{equation} \int f(x) \ dx = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} c_k \frac{x^{k+1}}{k+1} + C\tag{3.11} \end{equation}

    for \(|x| < R\text{.}\)
