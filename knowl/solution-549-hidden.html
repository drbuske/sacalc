1. Take the tangent of both sides. This gives \(\tan\theta = \tan(\pi/3) = \sqrt{3}\text{.}\) Since \(\tan\theta = \frac{y}{x}\) we can replace the left-hand side of this equation by \(\frac{y}{x}\text{.}\) This gives \(\frac{y}{x}=\sqrt{3}\text{,}\) which can be rewritten as \(y=\sqrt{3}x\text{.}\)

  2. First, square both sides of the equation. This gives \(r^2=9\text{.}\) Next replace \(r^2\) with \(x^2+y^2\text{.}\) This gives the equation \(x^2+y^2=9\text{,}\) which is the equation of a circle centered at the origin with radius 3. (Note: when squaring both sides of an equation it is possible to introduce new points unintentionally. This should always be taken into consideration. However, in this case we do not introduce new points. For example, \(\left(-3,\frac{\pi}{3}\right)\) is the same point as \(\left( 3,\frac{4\pi}{3}\right)\text{.}\))

  3. Multiply both sides of the equation by \(r\text{.}\) This leads to \(r^2=6r\cos\theta - 8r\sin\theta\text{.}\) Next use the formulas

    \begin{equation*} r^2=x^2+y^2, \ x=r\cos\theta, \ y=r\sin\theta\text{.} \end{equation*}

    This gives

    \begin{align*} r^2 = \mathstrut \amp 6(r\cos\theta) - 8(r\sin\theta)\\ x^2+y^2 = \mathstrut \amp 6x-8y. \end{align*}

    To put this equation into standard form, first move the variables from the right-hand side of the equation to the left-hand side, then complete the square.

    \begin{align*} x^2+y^2 = \mathstrut \amp 6x-8y\\ x^2-6x+y^2+8y = \mathstrut \amp 0\\ (x^2-6x)+(y^2+8y) = \mathstrut \amp 0\\ (x^2-6x+9)+(y^2+8y+16) = \mathstrut \amp 9+16\\ (x-3)^2+(y+4)^2 = \mathstrut \amp 25. \end{align*}

    This is the equation of a circle with center at \((3,-4)\) and radius \(5\text{.}\) Notice that the circle passes through the origin since the center is \(5\) units away.
