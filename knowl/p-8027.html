Since both series converge, we can apply the properties of convergent series to evaluate

\begin{align*} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left[ \frac{1}{n(n+1)} + \left( \frac{1}{2}\right)^{n-2} \right] = \mathstrut \amp \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n(n+1)} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left( \frac{1}{2}\right)^{n-2} \\ = \mathstrut \amp \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n(n+1)} + \left( \frac{1}{2}\right)^{-1} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left( \frac{1}{2}\right)^{n-1}\\ = \mathstrut \amp 1 + \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^{-1} (2)\\ = \mathstrut \amp 5. \end{align*}
