Item a.

Since \(x'(t)=2\) and \(y'(t)=3t^2-3\text{,}\) we have

\begin{align*} \frac{dy}{dx}= \mathstrut \amp \frac{dy/dt}{dx/dt}\\ =\mathstrut \amp \frac{3t^2-3}{2}. \end{align*}

This derivative is zero when \(t=\pm 1\text{.}\) When \(t=-1\) we have \(x(-1)=2(-1)+1=-1\) and \(y(-1) = (-1)^3 -3(-1)+4=6\) which corresponds to the point \((-1,6)\) on the graph. When \(t=1\) we have \(x(1)=2(1)+1=3\) and \(y(1)=(1)^3-3(1)+4=2\) which corresponds to the point \((3,2)\) on the graph. The point \((3,2)\) is a relative minimum and the point \((-1,6)\) is a relative maximum, as seen in FigureÂ 9.2.7.
