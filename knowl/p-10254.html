A polar curve \(r=f(\theta)\) can be written in parametric form

\begin{align*} x= \mathstrut \amp r\cos\theta = f(\theta)\cos\theta\\ y = \mathstrut \amp r\sin\theta = f(\theta)\sin\theta \end{align*}

where we treat \(\theta\) as the parameter. The derivative is given by

\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy/d\theta}{dx/d\theta}\text{.} \end{equation*}
