Imagine now taking the \(i\)th shell, making a vertical cut, and then opening it up to form a flat plate as in FigureÂ 6.2.12. In reality, the outer radius of the shell is greater than the inner radius, and hence the back edge of the plate would be slightly longer than the front edge of the plate. However, we can approximate the flattened shell by a flat plate of height \(f(x_i)\text{,}\) width \(2\pi x_i\text{,}\) and thickness \(\Delta x\text{.}\) The volume of the shell, then, is approximately the volume of the flat plate. Multiplying the height, width, and depth of the plate, we get

\begin{equation*} V_{shell} = f(x_i)(2\pi x_i) \Delta x\text{.} \end{equation*}
