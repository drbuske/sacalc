Answer 5.5.4.7.1.

The possible term \(\displaystyle \frac{A}{x-1}\) in the decomposition is missing. In fact, it turns out that
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{(x-1)^2(x-2)} = \frac{-1}{x-1} + \frac{-1}{(x-1)^2} + \frac{1}{x-2}. \end{equation*}
The correct format of the decomposition is
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \frac{A}{x-1} + \frac{B}{(x-1)^2} + \frac{C}{x-2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
