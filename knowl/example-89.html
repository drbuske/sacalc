Example 8.4.7.

The error function

\begin{equation*} \erf(x) = \frac{2}{\sqrt{\pi}} \int_0^x e^{-t^2} \ dt \end{equation*}

is used in computing "bell curve" probabilities. For example, for a normal distribution (i.e. one shaped like a bell curve), the probability of being within one standard deviation from the mean is \(\erf \left( \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \right)\text{.}\) Use a Maclaurin series for \(e^{-t^2}\) to evaluate \(\erf \left( \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\right)\) with an error of less than \(2 \times 10^{-5}\text{.}\)
