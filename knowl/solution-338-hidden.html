\begin{equation*} \frac{2x}{x^2-64}=\frac{A}{x+8}+\frac{B}{x-8} \end{equation*}
so that
\begin{equation*} 2x = A(x-8)+B(x+8) = (A+B)x + 8 (-A+B). \end{equation*}
Solving, we get \(A=B=1\text{,}\) so that
\begin{equation*} \int \frac{2x}{x^2-64}\,dx= \int \left(\frac{1}{x+8}+\frac{1}{x-8}\right)\,dx =\ln|x+8|+\ln|x-8|+C. \end{equation*}
Using the substitution \(w=x^2-64\text{,}\) we get \(dw= 2x\,dx\text{,}\) so we have
\begin{equation*} \int \frac{2x}{x^2-64}\,dx= \int \frac{dw}{w}=\ln|w|+C = \ln|x^2-64|+C. \end{equation*}
The properties of logarithms show that the two results are the same:
\begin{equation*} \ln|x+8|+\ln|x-8| = \ln|(x+8)(x-8)| =\ln|x^2-64|. \end{equation*}
