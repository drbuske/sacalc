Item a.

Recall that for large \(k\text{,}\) \(\frac{k+1}{k^3+2}\) looks like \(\frac{k}{k^3}\text{.}\) Let's formalize this observation a bit more. Let \(a_k = \frac{k+1}{k^3+2}\) and \(b_k = \frac{k}{k^3}\text{.}\) Calculate

\begin{equation*} \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{a_k}{b_k}\text{.} \end{equation*}

What does the value of the limit tell you about \(a_k\) and \(b_k\) for large values of \(k\text{?}\)
