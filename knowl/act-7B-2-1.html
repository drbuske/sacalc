Activity 9.2.2.

The path of a child on a merry-go-round is given by the parametric equations \(x = 4 \sin(t/2)\) and \(y = 4 \cos(t/2)\) where \(t\) is measured in seconds and \(x\) and \(y\) are measured in feet.

  1. Sketch the path of the child for \(0 \leq t \leq 6\pi\text{.}\) Be sure to place arrows on the path to denote direction.

  2. Where is the child located at time \(t=\frac{3\pi}{2}\text{?}\) Mark that location on the path with a \(\circ\text{.}\)

  3. Compute \(\frac{dx}{dt}\) and \(\frac{dy}{dt}\text{.}\) Without calculating, when \(t=\frac{3\pi}{2}\text{,}\) what do you expect the sign of each to be? Why?

  4. Evaluate \(x' \left( \frac{3\pi}{2} \right)\) and \(y' \left( \frac{3\pi}{2} \right)\text{.}\)

  5. Using the fact that \(\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy/dt}{dx/dt}\text{,}\) calculate the slope of the curve when \(t=\frac{3\pi}{2}\text{.}\)

  6. When the child is thrown from the merry-go-round, her (horizontal) path follows the tangent line. Determine the equation of the tangent line to the curve at time \(t=\frac{3\pi}{2}\text{.}\) Sketch this line on your graph above.
