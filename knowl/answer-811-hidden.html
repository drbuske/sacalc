1. Note that

    \begin{equation*} f'(x) = -x + \frac{x^3}{3!} - \frac{x^5}{5!} + \cdots + (-1)^k \frac{x^{2k-1}}{(2k-1)!} + \cdots \end{equation*}

  2. We recognize \(f(x)\) as \(\cos(x)\) and \(f'(x)\) as \(-\sin(x)\text{.}\) This gives the known differentiation formula \(\frac{d}{dx}\cos(x) = -\sin(x)\text{.}\)

  3. We see that

    \begin{equation*} f''(x) = -1 + \frac{x^2}{2!} - \frac{x^4}{4!} + \cdots \end{equation*}

    which is the series expansion for \(-\cos(x)\) as expected.
