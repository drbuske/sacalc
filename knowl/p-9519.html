That is, indeed we can differentiate a power series for a function \(f\) term by term and obtain the series expansion for \(f'\text{.}\) Similarly we can integrate a series expansion for a function \(f\) term by term and obtain the series expansion for \(\int f(x) \ dx\text{.}\) For both, the radius of convergence of the resulting series is the same as the original, though it is possible that the convergence status of the various series may differ at the endpoints.
