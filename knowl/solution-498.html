Solution 8.3.3.1.

From Preview ActivityÂ 8.3.1, we see that \(f^{(2k+1)}(0)=(-1)^k\) and \(f^{(2k)}(0)=0\text{.}\) So the MacLaurin series for \(\sin(x)\) is given by

\begin{equation*} T_f(x) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{f^{(k)}(0)}{k!}x^k = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^k}{(2k+1)!}x^{2k+1}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Writing out the first few terms gives \(T_f(x) = x - \frac{1}{3!}x^3 + \frac{1}{5!}x^5 - \frac{1}{7!}x^7 + \cdots\text{.}\)
