The Method of Cylindrical Shells.

Let \(f(x)\) be continuous and nonnegative. Define \(R\) as the region bounded above by the graph of \(f(x)\text{,}\) below by the \(x\)-axis, on the left by the line \(x=a\text{,}\) and on the right by the line \(x=b\text{.}\) Then the volume of the solid of revolution formed by revolving \(R\) around the \(y\)-axis is given by

\begin{equation} V = \int_a^b 2\pi x f(x) \ dx\text{.}\tag{6.4} \end{equation}
