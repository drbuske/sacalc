To find the inflection points, we find \(f''(x)\) and determine where it is zero.

\begin{equation*} f''(x) = 3( -2(4) e^{-4 x^2} + 4(4)^2 x^2 e^{-4 x^2} = 24 e^{-4 x^2} ( -1 + 8 x^2 ). \end{equation*}

For this to be zero, we must have \(-1 + 8 x^2 = 0\text{,}\) or \(x = \pm 1/\sqrt{8}\text{.}\) There are exactly two inflection points, at \(x = -1/\sqrt{8} \approx -0.354\) and \(x = 1/\sqrt{8} \approx 0.354\text{.}\)
