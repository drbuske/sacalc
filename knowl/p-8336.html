Use the direct comparison test to determine whether each series below converges or diverges. The following are necessary components of a complete solution.

  • Define \(a_k\text{,}\) and make a good choice for \(b_k\) to which you will compare it.

  • Confirm that both \(a_k \geq 0\) and \(b_k \geq 0\text{.}\)

  • Determine which is larger (for all \(n\) after some point), \(a_k\) or \(b_k\text{.}\)

  • Determine the convergence or divergence of \(\displaystyle \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} b_k\text{.}\) You must have a reason for this (e.g. \(p\)-series, integral test, divergence test).

  • Make a conclusion about the convergence or divergence of \(\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} a_k\text{.}\) Again, you must state a reason for this conclusion.

  1. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{e^{-k}}{k^2}\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{k-1}{k^3+3}\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{6k^2+1}{2k^3-1}\)
