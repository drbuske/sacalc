Solution 8.4.5.1.

The taylor series for \(\sin(x)\) about \(x=0\) is given by

\begin{equation*} \sin(x) = x - \frac{x^3}{3!} + \frac{x^5}{5!} - \cdots\text{.} \end{equation*}

Therefore

\begin{align*} \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\sin(x)}{x} \amp = \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{x - \frac{x^3}{3!} + \frac{x^5}{5!} - \cdots}{x}\\ \amp = \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} 1 - \frac{x^2}{3!} + \frac{x^4}{5!} - \cdots\\ \amp = 1 \end{align*}
