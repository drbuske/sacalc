Solution 7.5.4.2.1.

\(a_k=\frac{k^{5}}{k^{6}+3}\) and \(b_k=\frac{1}{k}\)\(\displaystyle\rho=\lim_{k\to\infty} \left(\frac{k^{6}}{k^{6}+3}\right)=1\text{,}\) so the series is Divergent, since \(b_k=\frac{1}{k}\) gives a divergent \(\small{p}\)-series. (b) Using the limit comparison test with \(a_k=\frac{\sqrt{k}}{k^{6}+3}\) and \(b_k=\frac{1}{\sqrt{k^{11}}}\)\(\displaystyle\rho=\lim_{k\to\infty} \left(\frac{k^{6}}{k^{6}+3}\right)=1\text{,}\) so the series is Convergent, since \(b_k=\frac{1}{\sqrt{k^{11}}}\) gives a convergent \(\small{p}\)-series.
