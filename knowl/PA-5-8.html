Preview Activity 5.7.1.

Suppose an angle \(\theta\) is such that \(0 < \theta < \frac{\pi}{2}\) as shown in 5.7.1. Further, suppose it is known that \(\sin \theta = \frac{x}{a}\text{.}\)

  1. Label the remaining sides of the right triangle shown in 5.7.1.

  2. Determine the values of \(\cos\theta, \tan\theta, \cot\theta, \sec\theta\text{,}\) and \(\csc\theta\) in terms of \(x\) and \(a\text{.}\)

  3. Determine the value of \(\cos^2(\theta)\sin(\theta)\) in terms of \(x\) and \(a\)

  4. Write the expression \(\displaystyle \frac{x^3}{a^2\sqrt{a^2-x^2}}\) in terms of \(\theta\text{.}\)
