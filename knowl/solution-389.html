Solution 6.2.17.1.

Notice that every \(z\)-cross-section (i.e. slice parallel to the \(xy\)-plane) is a square, so we will want to integrate with respect to \(z\text{.}\)

Slicing the pyramid at a \(z\)-value (height) between 0 and 2 gives a square shape having area \(A(z)\text{.}\) We determine a formula for \(A(z)\text{.}\) We start by finding a function \(f(z)\) that describes the side-length of this square. Slicing at \(z=0\) gives the base of the pyramid, which is a square of side length 1. So \(f(0)=1\text{.}\) Slicing at \(z=2\) gives the peak of the pyramid, which can be thought of as a square of side length 0, so \(f(2)=0\text{.}\) Therefore this linear function will have intercept \(b=1\) and slope \(m=\frac{0-1}{2-0} = -\frac{1}{2}\text{,}\) so \(f(z) = -\frac{1}{2}z+1\text{.}\) Thus the area of a square at height \(z\) is

\begin{equation*} A(z) = \left(-\frac{1}{2}z+1\right)^2\text{.} \end{equation*}

Integrating from the bottom to the top of the pyramid gives

\begin{align*} {\text{Volume}} = \mathstrut \amp \int_a^b A(z) \ dz \\ = \mathstrut \amp \int_0^2 \left( -\frac{1}{2}z + 1 \right)^2 \ dz\\ = \mathstrut \amp \frac{2}{3}. \end{align*}

It is useful to compare this answer to one that can be found through the use of a known formula for the volume of a pyramid: \(V=\frac{1}{3}l^2h\) where \(l\) is the side length of the base and \(h\) is the height. Using this formula gives a volume of \(V = \frac{1}{3}l^2h = \frac{1}{3}(1)^2(2) = \frac{2}{3}\) as anticipated.
