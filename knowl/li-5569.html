Item b.

For all \(y\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} \sin y = y - \frac{1}{3!}y^3 + \frac{1}{5!}y^5 - \cdots\text{.} \end{equation*}

Substituting \(y=2x^3\text{,}\) we have

\begin{align*} \sin(2x^3) \amp = 2x^3 - \frac{1}{3!}(2x^3)^3 + \frac{1}{5!} (2x^3)^5 - \cdots\\ \amp = 2x^3 - \frac{8}{3!}x^9 + \frac{2^5}{5!}x^{15} - \cdots \end{align*}

So the coefficient of \(x^{15}\) in the Maclaurin series of \(f(x)=\sin(2x^3)\) is \(\frac{2^5}{5!}\text{.}\)
