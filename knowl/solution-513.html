Solution 8.3.5.9.1.

(a) In this case the graph of \(E_1 = \ln\!\left(x+1\right) - (x)\) looks like a parabola, which makes sense because we know that the error bound for Taylor polynomials gives \(E_1 \propto x^2\text{.}\) In fact, because
\begin{equation*} |E_1(x)| \le \frac{M}{(2)!}|x|^{2}, \end{equation*}
where \(M\) is a good error bound for \(f''(x)\text{,}\) and knowing that \(|f''(x)| = \frac{1}{(x+1)^2} \le \frac{1}{(0.9)^2}\) on \(-0.1\le x\le 0.1\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} |E_1(x)| \le \frac{x^{2}}{2\cdot 0.9^{2}}. \end{equation*}
A graph of \(|E_1| = |\ln\!\left(x+1\right) - (x)|\) and \(\frac{x^{2}}{2\cdot 0.9^{2}}\) is shown below.
(b) In this case the graph of \(E_2 = \ln\!\left(x+1\right) - (x-\frac{x^{2}}{2})\) looks like a cubic, which makes sense because we know that the error bound for Taylor polynomials gives \(E_2 \propto x^3\text{.}\) As above, because
\begin{equation*} |E_2(x)| \le \frac{M}{(3)!}|x|^{3}, \end{equation*}
where \(M\) is a good error bound for \(f'''(x)\text{,}\) and knowing that \(|f'''(x)| = \frac{2}{(x+1)^3} \le \frac{2}{(0.9)^3}\) on \(-0.1\le x\le 0.1\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} |E_2(x)| \le \frac{\left|x\right|^{3}}{3\cdot 0.9^{3}}. \end{equation*}
A graph of \(|E_2| = |\ln\!\left(x+1\right) - (x-\frac{x^{2}}{2})|\) and \(\frac{\left|x\right|^{3}}{3\cdot 0.9^{3}}\) is shown below.
