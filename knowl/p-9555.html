In this activity, we quickly calculate the value of \(f^{(15)}(0)\text{,}\) the fifteenth derivative of \(f(x) = \sin (2x^3)\) at \(x=0\text{.}\)

  1. For a general Taylor series of \(f(x)\) about \(x=0\text{,}\)

    \begin{equation*} f(x)=\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{f^{(k)}(0)}{k!} x^k\text{.} \end{equation*}

    In this series, what is the coefficient of \(x^{15}\text{?}\)

  2. Find the the first three terms of the Taylor series for \(f(x) = \sin(2x^3)\) about \(x=0\text{.}\) What is the coefficient of \(x^{15}\) in this expression?

  3. Use your answers to (a) and (b) to determine the value of \(f^{(15)}(0)\) when \(f(x) = \sin(2x^3)\text{.}\)
