When identifying variables and drawing a picture, it is important to think about the dynamic ways in which the quantities change. Sometimes a sequence of pictures can be helpful; for some pictures that can be easily modified as applets built in Geogebra, see the following links, 45  which represent

  • how a circular oil slick's area grows as its radius increases http://gvsu.edu/s/9n 47 ;

  • how the location of the base of a ladder and its height along a wall change as the ladder slides http://gvsu.edu/s/9o 48 ;

  • how the water level changes in a conical tank as it fills with water at a constant rate http://gvsu.edu/s/9p 49  (compare the problem in Activity 3.5.2);

  • how a skateboarder's shadow changes as he moves past a lamppost http://gvsu.edu/s/9q 50 .
