Solution 8.3.5.1.1.

\(\displaystyle \frac{5}{x}\) and its first three derivatives are \(f(x) = \displaystyle \frac{5}{x}\text{,}\) \(f'(x) = -{5\over x^2}\text{,}\) \(f''(x) = {10\over x^3}\text{,}\) and \(f'''(x) = -{30\over x^4}\text{.}\) Thus, evaluating these at \(x = -2\text{,}\) we get the terms term 0 = \({{5\over-2}}\) term 1 = \(-{{5\over4}}(x + 2)\) term 2 = \({{5\over-8}}(x + 2)^2\text{,}\) and term 3 = \(-{{5\over16}}(x + 2)^3\text{.}\) Thus the series is
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \frac{5}{x} = {{5\over-2}} - {{5\over4}}(x + 2) + {{5\over-8}}(x + 2)^2 - {{5\over16}}(x + 2)^3 + \cdots. \end{equation*}
