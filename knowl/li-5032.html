Here \(a_k = \frac{(-1)^k(k!)^2}{(2k)!}\text{.}\) So,

\begin{equation*} \frac{a_{k+1}}{a_k} = \frac{(-1)^{k+1}[(k+1)!]^2}{[2(k+1)]!} \cdot \frac{(2k)!}{(-1)^k(k!)^2} = \frac{(-1)(k+1)^2}{(2k+2)(2k+1)} = -\frac{k^2+2k+1}{4k^2+6k+2} \rightarrow -\frac{1}{4} \end{equation*}

as \(k \rightarrow \infty\text{.}\) By the ratio test, the series converges and behaves like a geometric series with common ratio \(r=-\frac{1}{4}\text{.}\)
