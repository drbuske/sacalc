Solution 9.2.19.1.

Suppose that \(x=x(t), y=y(t), t_1 \leq t \leq t_2\) parameterizes the curve whose graph is \(y=f(x)\) between \(x=a\) and \(x=b\text{.}\) Then \(y(t)=f(x(t))\) and the chain rule gives \(y'(t)=f'(x(t))x'(t)\text{.}\) Using equation (9.5) gives

\begin{align*} L = \mathstrut \amp \int_{t_1}^{t_2} \sqrt{ \left( \frac{dx}{dt} \right)^2 + \left( \frac{dy}{dt} \right)^2} \ dt\\ \mathstrut \amp \int_{t_1}^{t_2} \sqrt{ \left( \frac{dx}{dt} \right)^2 + \left( f'(x)\frac{dx}{dt} \right)^2} \ dt\\ \mathstrut \amp \int_{t_1}^{t_2} \sqrt{ \left( \frac{dx}{dt} \right)^2 \left( 1+ f'(x)^2 \right)} \ dt\\ \mathstrut \amp \int_{t_1}^{t_2} x'(t) \sqrt{\left( 1+ f'(x)^2 \right)} \ dt \\ \mathstrut \amp \int_{a}^{b} \sqrt{\left( 1+ f'(x)^2 \right)} \ dx \end{align*}

where we have \(a=x(t_1)\text{,}\) \(b=x(t_2)\text{,}\) and we have assumed that \(x'(t)>0\) so that \(dx = x'(t)\ dt\text{.}\) Of course this is the formula from Section 6.1.
