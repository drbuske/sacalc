On the other hand, if a series \(\sum b_k\) has terms that are all (beyond a certain \(k\)) larger than the corresponding terms in a series \(\sum a_k\) that we know diverges, then the series \(\sum b_k\) must also diverge, because it is "larger" than something infinite. Thus, we can use the second bullet point to prove that a series (\(\sum b_n\)) diverges.
